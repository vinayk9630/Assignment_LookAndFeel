LIVING WITH EPILEPSY:

Understanding Epilepsy

Epilepsy is a serious brain ailment that is a common neurological disorder across the world. It is a clinical outcome associated with abnormal neurological electrical discharge of the brain. Epileptic seizures are among the most widespread positive indications and symptoms of brain disruption, and epilepsy is a major brain condition. Seizures and epilepsy are among the most frequent neurological disorders affecting children and adolescents. There are 470,000 children with epilepsy in the United States. That means at least six students in a 1,000-student school are likely to have epilepsy.
