LIVING WITH EPILEPSY

Understanding Epilepsy

Epilepsy is a serious brain ailment that is a common neurological disorder across the world. It is characterized by abnormal neurological electrical discharges in the brain. Epileptic seizures are among the most widespread positive indications and symptoms of brain disruption, making epilepsy a significant brain condition. Notably, seizures and epilepsy are among the most frequent neurological disorders affecting children and adolescents, with approximately 470,000 children diagnosed in the United States alone.

Understanding EEG and Its Role in Epilepsy Diagnosis

Electroencephalography (EEG) is a medical technique used to measure the electrical activity of the brain via sensors placed on the scalp. It is critical in diagnosing neurological disorders, particularly epilepsy, by detecting abnormalities in brain waves that indicate seizures.

About the CHB-MIT EEG Dataset

The CHB-MIT EEG dataset is a collection of EEG recordings from pediatric subjects with epilepsy, specifically designed for the development and testing of automated seizure detection systems. This dataset includes extensive EEG data and is a vital resource for researchers in the field.

Applying Machine Learning to Seizure Detection

Advanced machine learning and deep learning techniques, such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), are increasingly used to analyze EEG data for seizure detection. These models can learn to recognize patterns associated with seizures, offering significant improvements over traditional methods.

Studies and implementations on platforms like GitHub demonstrate the effectiveness of these technologies in real-world applications.
